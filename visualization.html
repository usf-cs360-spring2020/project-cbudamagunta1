Final Visualization

Innovation Rates per State dependant on Parent Quintile and Gender

Purpose

This choropleth map looks at the specific distribution of innovation rates for different states based on the selected variable, providing a closer look at how these rates change dependant on different factors. The bar chart allows for comparison between all factors to see which contribute to the highest rates. This is an exploratory visualization to further look into the factors that most affect one's likelihood of becoming an inventor, to therefore understand what support needs to be provided to those who do not have those advantages.

Encoding

The map uses a quantize color scale to encode the inventor rate based on the selected variable per state. Darker colors indicate higher rates.

The bar chart, shown when hovering over a state, displays the rate across all variables for the highlighted state. Taller bars indicate higher rates.


Dataset Used:

Origins of Inventors: Innovation Rates by Childhood State, Gender, and Parent Income

There was no wrangling of the data needed.

See Data page for more info

Columns:

  • par_state: childhood state of residence
  • par_stateabbrv: state abbreviation
  • inventor: share of children overall who go on to become inventors
  • inventor_g_m: same as inventor but restricted to males
  • inventor_g_f: same as inventor but restricted to females
  • inventor_pq_1: same as inventor but restricted to children whose parental income is in quintile 1 of the parent income distribution of the children's birth cohort
  • inventor_pq_2: same as inventor but restricted to children whose parental income is in quintile 2 of the parent income distribution of the children's birth cohort
  • inventor_pq_3: same as inventor but restricted to children whose parental income is in quintile 3 of the parent income distribution of the children's birth cohort
  • inventor_pq_4: same as inventor but restricted to children whose parental income is in quintile 4 of the parent income distribution of the children's birth cohort
  • inventor_pq_5: same as inventor but restricted to children whose parental income is in quintile 5 of the parent income distribution of the children's birth cohort
  • kid_count: number of children

Interactivity

Filtering: Choose a data variable using the dropdown at the top to re-render the map with that specific information.

Highlighting: Hover over a state to bring it forward and outline it.

Details-on-Demand: Hover over a state to display the state name, specific value for the selected variable, and number of kids in that cohort. This will also display a bar chart listing all conditional inventor rates for that state.

Brushing: Hover over a cell on the legend to highlight all states that fall under that category.

Findings

This visualization reveals a lot of patterns in the data. One finding that can be seen is that, for every state, the shape of the bar chart remains similar, meaning all the rates are in similar relation to each other across the board. The highest rates tend to be in the subsection of children with parents in the 5th quintile, and with children in the male category. The lowest rates tend to be in the subsection of children with parents in the 1st quintile, and with children in the female category. This seems consistant with the idea that children who come from wealthy parents will have a better chance at acheiving inventor status, and children who are classified as male have a better chance than those classified as female. Based on these findings then, in order to boost inventor rates, policies should focus on increasing opportunities to lower income households and encouraging more girls to pursue inventor status.
