Who Becomes an Inventor in America? The Importance of Exposure to Innovation

The Who Becomes an Inventor in America? The Importance of Exposure to Innovation project from Opportunity Insights looks at the key factors amongst individuals who become inventors in the United States, as measured by filing a patent. They look at different conditions that encouraged innovation in these individuals, in order to determine what policies would be most effective at sparking that innovation in other, underrepresented populations.

The projects consists of 7 different datasets, each focusing on a different area within the overall subject. The project was accessed on April 11th, 2020.

Specific Datasets

Within this project, the specific datasets used in these visualizations are:

  1. Origins of Inventors: Innovation Rates by Childhood State, Gender, and Parent Income
  2. Careers of Inventors: Innovation Rates by Current State, Gender, Year of Birth, and Age
  3. Innovation Rates by College

1 and 3 share the same birth cohort, 1980-1984.

Origins of Inventors: Innovation Rates by Childhood State, Gender, and Parent Income

This dataset looks at patenting outcomes for children born in 1980-1984 by the state in which they grew up, gender, and parent income.

Innovation Rates by College

This dataset provides estimates of patenting rates based on college attended. They use regression models to pool data across several colleges to provide data with a smaller degree of error than the degree of sampling error.
